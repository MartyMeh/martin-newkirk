2 2014-2024 UAT & Quality Assurance Process
Photonik & Car Next Door		 Situation
Needed to establish robust user acceptance testing practices across both Photonik and Car Next Door. Photonik required rigorous testing of regulatory compliance features (government scheme calculations like STCs) with installers across multiple countries. Car Next Door needed appropriate UAT processes for agile development environment without heavy waterfall overhead.
 Task
Establish and lead user acceptance testing practices that ensure quality and compliance while fitting agile development workflows.
 Action
Photonik: Recruited and coordinated 3-4 installers across different countries (AU, ZA, US) to test features on staging pre-release. Implemented step-by-step verification of government scheme calculations against regulator requirements, with automated tests and detailed logging. Built in-app bug reporting system, daily analytics monitoring, and structured error tracking.

Car Next Door: Introduced STAFF user group for early production access and beta testing. Extended to select heavy users as beta community. Coordinated operations team testing. Engaged Head of Legal to review compliance features pre-deployment. Implemented prioritized bug tracking (P0-P3) with clear escalation protocols - P0 bugs triggered all-hands response.
 Result
Established appropriate UAT for agile environments with phased rollouts, beta testing, compliance verification, and post-release monitoring. Demonstrated ability to balance quality assurance with agile development speed.
3 2024 Vehicle Safety Policy Framework Overhaul
Uber Carshare		 Situation
As Policy, Safety & Risk Programs Lead, identified that vehicle safety policies had evolved piecemeal over time, creating inconsistencies and gaps. Deactivation frameworks weren't consistently applied, and we lacked a unified safety dashboard to monitor fleet-wide safety performance. In peer-to-peer carsharing, safety inconsistency creates both user risk and regulatory exposure - particularly critical given Uber's very high safety standards (their #1 value after learning hard lessons in early years).
 Task
Overhaul the entire vehicle safety policy framework to ensure consistency, improve monitoring, and enhance proactive safety risk management.
 Action
Conducted thorough review of existing safety policies to identify gaps and inconsistencies. Designed new deactivation frameworks with clear criteria and escalation pathways. Implemented safety dashboard providing real-time visibility into fleet safety metrics, enabling proactive identification of issues before they became critical. Coordinated review and resolution processes for critical safety issues across the fleet. Developed training and communication materials for operations teams. Established regular review cadence to ensure policies remained current and responsive to emerging risks.
 Result
Created consistent, defensible safety policy framework aligned with Uber's standards. Safety dashboard enabled proactive issue identification and faster response times. Reduced regulatory risk through better documentation and consistency. Built foundation for safer fleet and better user protection. Relevance to Ofgem: Experience managing safety and compliance frameworks in regulated environment directly prepares for Ofgem context, where products must meet statutory obligations and protect vulnerable consumers. Safety dashboard approach mirrors KPI monitoring essential for scheme administration products.
4 2023 AFCA Complaints Compliance System
Car Next Door / Uber Carshare		 Situation
Company was deemed to offer a financial product (insurance when borrowers rented cars) and was non-compliant with Australia's financial regulator (AFCA - Australian Financial Complaints Authority) requirements. Under heightened Uber brand scrutiny, we needed urgent compliance work. Race against time with competing resource constraints - frontline capacity and development resources were also needed for growth initiatives.
 Task
Build comprehensive complaints system to meet regulator requirements while managing competing business priorities. Essential compliance work requiring both speed AND quality.
 Action
Built comprehensive complaints system including: policy framework, functionality for customers to complain at every transaction point, recording systems, escalation pathways up to the ombudsman, analytics dashboards, and frontline training. Coordinated between compliance requirements and business growth needs, managing stakeholder expectations and resource allocation.
 Result
Delivered compliant system meeting regulator requirements while balancing competing priorities. Why this is powerful for Ofgem: Direct experience being regulated, understanding ombudsman escalation processes, building systems to meet regulator requirements while managing competing business priorities.
7 2018+ Agile & OKR Implementation
Car Next Door		 Situation
As Car Next Door's first Product Manager, the company lacked structured development processes and goal-setting frameworks. Needed to establish agile development processes and OKR framework from scratch to enable better planning, execution, and alignment.
 Task
Establish agile development processes and OKR framework that fit the organization's context and enable better product delivery and strategic alignment.
 Action
Conducted extensive research: consulted with experienced internal developers, read multiple books on Scrum and agile practices, and most valuably - found and met with experts at leading tech companies (Tech Lead at Atlassian, Product Manager at Google). Had multiple in-depth discussions to understand how successful organizations implement agile and OKRs. Synthesized learnings and worked with leadership team and broader organization to design and implement tailored processes. Continued to lead and evolve OKR process company-wide through 2022+, including executive approval cycles, strategy setting, quarterly planning, and retrospectives.
 Result
Established processes from scratch through research, learning from best practices, and adapting to organizational context. Demonstrated initiative in seeking out experts and translating external knowledge to internal implementation. Created framework that enabled better strategic alignment and product delivery.
8 2020-2021 Key Swap Product Launch
New Carshare Onboarding Model		 Situation
Faced critical business constraints: high theft risk (up to 5% of fleet stolen per quarter), owner growth barriers limiting trip growth, and installation friction preventing scale. The existing "Instant Key" model required full device installation on every car, with keys stored in lockboxes on vehicles - creating both security risks and operational bottlenecks.
 Task
Develop an entirely new carshare product offering to address theft risk, owner growth barriers, and installation friction while maintaining platform viability.
 Action
Proposed and championed the lightest-touch approach: Key Swap required manual key exchanges (more friction per trip) but eliminated installation requirements and theft risk, dramatically reducing signup friction. Strategic hypothesis: get many more owners on platform quickly, then convert high-performing ones to Instant Key while moving poor performers off. Complete overhaul of onboarding flow, admin site, and booking flow - essentially building a new product into existing system. Coordinated between Product, Engineering, Design, and Operations teams. Eliminated manual sales and onboarding processes (owners self-onboarded), shifted installation team to sending kits, conducted thorough testing. Tracked owner signup rates, car utilization rates, owner ratings, and customer service call volumes. Faced challenges: initial low utilization and owner earnings led to disillusionment, increased customer service calls. Implemented solutions: monitored and removed poor-performing owners, encouraged lockboxes to reduce friction, developed and rolled out self-installation kits.
 Result
Despite initial disruption, Key Swap became essential to company growth. By Uber's acquisition, reached 10,000 cars with 60% on Key Swap. Product was instrumental in demonstrating growth trajectory and scale that attracted acquisition interest - without it, company likely wouldn't have reached numbers needed to be attractive acquisition target.
9 2018-2019 Strategic Project Prioritization
SEO vs Checkout Optimization		 Situation
As Growth Lead, faced a critical decision: with a small team and limited capacity, we could only focus on one major growth initiative for the quarter - either SEO growth work or checkout optimization/conversion improvements. Both projects promised significant impact, but required full team focus, meaning choosing one meant delaying the other.
 Task
Make a data-driven recommendation to the Leadership team about which key project we would focus on that quarter, with clear reasoning for why one option was better than another.
 Action
Gathered comprehensive data: analyzed existing SEO leads and their conversion rates, consulted with SEO experts to understand potential upside and time-to-value, worked closely with UX designer to assess size of conversion opportunities in checkout flow. Both initiatives would generate 'free' leads (organic SEO traffic vs improved conversion of existing traffic), so decision required careful comparison of impact, timing, and strategic value.
 Result
Proposed prioritizing SEO work because: (a) SEO requires time to compound and grow, so starting earlier maximized long-term value, and (b) strategically, it was better to expand the top of the funnel first - getting more users into the system - before optimizing conversion rates. We ultimately delivered both projects, but the strategic sequencing decision ensured we maximized impact given resource constraints.
