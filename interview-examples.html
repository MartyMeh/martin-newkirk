Policy, Safety & Risk Programs Lead (Uber Carshare)
Maps to:Making Effective Decisions, Changing and Improving, Communicating and Influencing, Stakeholder Management, Data-driven KPIs

Overhaul the entire vehicle safety policy framework to ensure consistency, improve monitoring, and enhance proactive safety risk management.

  • Obtained stakeholder sign-off on policy framework before implementation, ensuring all parties understood and agreed to the new approach
  • Built safety dashboard using Looker for database analysis and dashboard creation, providing real-time visibility into fleet safety metrics and enabling proactive identification of issues before they became critical
  • Coordinated review and resolution processes for critical safety issues across the fleet
  • Developed training and communication materials for operations teams
  • Established regular review cadence to ensure policies remained current and responsive to emerging risks
Result
  • Created consistent, defensible safety policy framework aligned with Uber's standards
  • Safety dashboard enabled proactive issue identification and faster response times
  • Reduced regulatory risk through better documentation and consistency
  • Built foundation for safer fleet and better user protection
  • Relevance to Ofgem: Experience managing safety and compliance frameworks in regulated environment directly prepares for Ofgem context, where products must meet statutory obligations and protect vulnerable consumers. Safety dashboard approach mirrors KPI monitoring essential for scheme administration products

2020-2021
Key Swap Product Launch
Product Manager (Car Next Door)
Maps to:Making Effective Decisions, Changing and Improving, Stakeholder Management, Data-driven KPIs, Delivering at Pace, Communicating and Influencing
Relevance:Demonstrates ability to lead major product initiatives, deliver transformative results, and manage complex stakeholder relationships while balancing competing priorities.
Situation
  • Faced critical business constraints: high theft risk (up to 5% of fleet stolen per quarter), owner growth barriers limiting trip growth, and installation friction preventing scale
  • The existing "Instant Key" model required full device installation on every car, with keys stored in lockboxes on vehicles - creating both security risks and operational bottlenecks
Task
Develop an entirely new carshare product offering to address theft risk, owner growth barriers, and installation friction while maintaining platform viability.
Action
  • Conducted cost/benefit analysis comparing approaches: evaluated trade-offs between installation costs, theft risk, operational overhead, and growth potential to determine optimal product strategy
  • Proposed and championed the lightest-touch approach: Key Swap required manual key exchanges (more friction per trip) but eliminated installation requirements and theft risk, dramatically reducing signup friction - analysis showed this would maximize owner acquisition despite higher per-trip friction
  • Strategic hypothesis: get many more owners on platform quickly, then convert high-performing ones to Instant Key while moving poor performers off
  • Complete overhaul of onboarding flow, admin site, and booking flow - essentially building a new product into existing system
  • Coordinated between Product, Engineering, Design, and Operations teams
  • Planned system cut-over strategy: phased rollout approach, coordinated with operations team to ensure smooth transition from old to new onboarding model
  • Developed comprehensive user training materials and conducted training sessions for operations team and customer support staff before launch
  • Eliminated manual sales and onboarding processes (owners self-onboarded), shifted installation team to sending kits, conducted thorough testing
  • Tracked owner signup rates, car utilization rates, owner ratings, and customer service call volumes using Mixpanel for funnel conversion analysis
  • Provided comprehensive post-launch support: dedicated support channels, rapid response to issues, regular check-ins with early adopters, and iterative improvements based on feedback
  • Faced challenges: initial low utilization and owner earnings led to disillusionment, increased customer service calls
  • Implemented solutions: monitored and removed poor-performing owners, encouraged lockboxes to reduce friction, developed and rolled out self-installation kits
Result
  • Despite initial disruption, Key Swap became essential to company growth
  • By Uber's acquisition, reached 10,000 cars with 60% on Key Swap
  • Product was instrumental in demonstrating growth trajectory and scale that attracted acquisition interest - without it, company likely wouldn't have reached numbers needed to be attractive acquisition target

2018-2019
Strategic Project Prioritization
Growth Lead (Car Next Door)
Maps to:Making Effective Decisions, Data-driven KPIs, Stakeholder Management, Communicating and Influencing
Relevance:Demonstrates strategic thinking and data-driven prioritization essential for managing competing initiatives in government context with limited resources.
Situation
  • As Growth Lead, faced a critical decision: with a small team and limited capacity, we could only focus on one major growth initiative for the quarter
  • Either SEO growth work or checkout optimization/conversion improvements - both projects promised significant impact
  • Required full team focus, meaning choosing one meant delaying the other
Task
Make a data-driven recommendation to the Leadership team about which key project we would focus on that quarter, with clear reasoning for why one option was better than another.
Action
  • Gathered comprehensive data: analyzed existing SEO leads and their conversion rates
  • Consulted with SEO experts to understand potential upside and time-to-value
  • Worked closely with UX designer to assess size of conversion opportunities in checkout flow
  • Both initiatives would generate 'free' leads (organic SEO traffic vs improved conversion of existing traffic), so decision required careful comparison of impact, timing, and strategic value
Result
  • Proposed prioritizing SEO work because: (a) SEO requires time to compound and grow, so starting earlier maximized long-term value, and (b) strategically, it was better to expand the top of the funnel first - getting more users into the system - before optimizing conversion rates
  • We ultimately delivered both projects, but the strategic sequencing decision ensured we maximized impact given resource constraints
